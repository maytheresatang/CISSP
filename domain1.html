The CIA Triad

🔒 Confidentiality

Prevent unauthorised disclosure. Controls: encryption, access control, classification, NDA.

Attack: snooping, eavesdropping, shoulder surfing, data theft

✅ Integrity

Prevent unauthorised modification. Controls: hashing (MD5, SHA), digital signatures, checksums, version control.

Attack: data tampering, man-in-the-middle, malware

📶 Availability

Ensure authorised users can access resources when needed. Controls: redundancy, failover, RAID, load balancing, DDoS mitigation, BCP/DRP.

Attack: DoS/DDoS, ransomware, natural disaster, hardware failure

Extended Security Concepts
ConceptDefinition
Non-repudiationProof that a party performed an action (sender cannot deny). Achieved via digital signatures.
AuthenticityVerifying identity of a user or system (who you say you are).
PrivacyRight of individuals to control personal information.
AccountabilityTracking actions back to a specific identity (audit logs).
⚠️ Exam Trap: Availability vs Confidentiality Priority In most civilian/commercial scenarios, Confidentiality > Integrity > Availability. In safety-critical systems (hospitals, power grids, OT/ICS) Availability often trumps Confidentiality. Read the scenario carefully.
Risk Formula Essentials
Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Asset Value
SLE (Single Loss Expectancy) = Asset Value × Exposure Factor (EF) ARO (Annual Rate of Occurrence) = how often per year ALE (Annual Loss Expectancy) = SLE × ARO ALE after control = ALE before – ALE after – Cost of control
📌 Worked Example Asset value: $200,000. Fire destroys 40% → EF = 0.4 → SLE = $80,000. Fire occurs once in 5 years → ARO = 0.2. ALE = $80,000 × 0.2 = $16,000/year. A sprinkler system costing $10,000/year that reduces ALE to $2,000 saves $4,000 net — worth implementing.

⚠️ Exam Trap: Residual vs Total Risk Total Risk = Threats × Vulnerabilities × Asset Value (no controls). Residual Risk = what remains AFTER controls. The exam often asks about residual risk — always "after controls are applied."

ISC2 Code of Ethics — Canon Order (CRITICAL)
🚨 Most Missed Question — Memorise This Order Canons are listed in PRIORITY order. If two canons conflict, the higher-numbered one yields to the lower.
Mnemonic
P · C · P · S
Protect Society → Act Honourably (Client) → Provide Competent Service (Profession) → Advance the Profession (Self)
#CanonWhat it means
1Protect society, the common good, necessary public trust and confidence, and the infrastructurePublic safety FIRST — above all clients and employers
2Act honourably, honestly, justly, responsibly, and legallyIntegrity in all professional dealings
3Provide diligent and competent service to principalsCompetence and diligence for clients/employers
4Advance and protect the professionSelf and profession last
⚠️ Exam Trap: Ethics in Action If you discover your employer is committing fraud, you do not handle it internally first. Canon 1 (public good) supersedes Canon 3 (client service). You must escalate externally if public harm is involved. Do NOT destroy evidence — preserve it.

BCP vs DRP — Key Distinction

📋 BCP (Business Continuity Plan)

  • Keeps business operating during disruption
  • Business process focus
  • Strategic / organisational
  • Longer time horizon
  • Includes DRP as a sub-component

🔧 DRP (Disaster Recovery Plan)

  • Restores IT systems after disruption
  • Technical / IT focus
  • Tactical / operational
  • Shorter time horizon
  • Sub-plan within BCP
Critical BCP/DRP Terminology
TermDefinitionExam Tip
RTO (Recovery Time Objective)Max acceptable downtime before service must be restoredRTO drives your recovery strategy choice
RPO (Recovery Point Objective)Max acceptable data loss measured in time (how old can your backup be?)RPO drives backup frequency
MTD / MAD (Maximum Tolerable / Allowable Downtime)Absolute maximum time before irreparable harmMTD > RTO always. If RTO exceeds MTD = failure.
MTTR (Mean Time To Repair)Average time to fix a failed componentLower is better
MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)Average time between failures of a systemHigher is better
WRT (Work Recovery Time)Time to restore business functions after systems are backRTO + WRT ≤ MTD

BCP Process Phases
  1. Project Initiation — Scope, policy, senior management support, BCP committee
  2. BIA (Business Impact Analysis) — Identify critical processes, dependencies, MTD, RTO, RPO, financial impact
  3. Recovery Strategy Selection — Choose controls and alternate sites based on BIA output
  4. Plan Development — Document procedures, assign roles, establish communication trees
  5. Testing & Maintenance — Test types below; review annually or after major change

✅ BCM Advantage: You already know this The BIA output drives everything: MTD → site selection, RPO → backup frequency, RTO → recovery strategy. The CISSP frames this exactly as you use it in your BCM role.
